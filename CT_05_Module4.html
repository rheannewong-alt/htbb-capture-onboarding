Module 04

Guidelines &
Do's & Don'ts

Being on the Capture Team means you're present for deeply personal moments. This module covers how to serve with grace, discretion, and respect.

Dress code

Wear loose-fitting, dark clothing — black is the preference, but any dark colour works. You'll be moving around a fair amount, so comfort matters as much as colour.

Before the service, pick up your Photo Team lanyard from the Production booth and wear it throughout. This lets the congregation know you're serving on duty — not just someone wandering around with a camera.

Why dark clothes?

You are a discreet observer. The focus belongs on what's happening on stage — not on you. Dark clothing keeps you out of the frame and out of people's attention. It's one of the simplest ways to serve well.

Movement & visibility

Your movement during the service should be intentional and minimal. Think of yourself as a presence that serves without disrupting.

Do
Use the back of the room, side aisles, and edges to capture moments. Move slowly and quietly, especially during worship and the message.
Don't
Walk in front of the stage, cut through the congregation mid-service, or stand in anyone's sightline for extended periods.
Do
Plan your positions before the service starts — know where you'll move and when, based on the service flow.
Don't
Use flash photography at any point during the service. Ever. It's disruptive and distracting, full stop.

Prayer ministry

When prayer ministry begins — put the camera down. This is a private and personal time for many people in the congregation. Photography during prayer ministry is not permitted under any circumstances.

Never photograph
Anyone who appears emotional, distressed, or is engaged in private prayer or conversation — even if the photo looks powerful. The person's dignity always comes first.
Never shoot from a distance to avoid being noticed
A telephoto lens doesn't make it respectful. If it's a moment you wouldn't photograph openly, don't photograph it at all.
Instead
Step back, put the camera away, and join the moment as a participant. The service is for you too.

Consent & children

HTBB parents are informed that photography takes place during Sunday services and have consented to their children being photographed in this context. You may photograph children freely during the service.

Do
Capture natural moments with children — joy, worship, connection. These make some of the most powerful images in our gallery.
Don't
Photograph children where their name tags, school uniforms with names, or any identifying personal information is visible in the frame.
If you're ever unsure

Ask the Sunday Lead before the service if you're unsure about a particular situation. It's always better to check than to capture something that needs to be deleted later. When in doubt, don't shoot.
