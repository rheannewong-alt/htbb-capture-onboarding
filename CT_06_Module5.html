Module 05

What to Expect
on Sundays

Your first Sunday doesn't have to feel like a scramble. Here's everything you need to know before you walk through the door.

Planning Center

Scheduling for the Capture Team is managed through Planning Center. When you're rostered on for a Sunday, you'll receive a notification via Planning Center. Please accept or decline your slot as soon as you see it — this helps the lead plan coverage in advance.

If you need to swap a Sunday or can't make it, reach out to the Capture Team Lead directly and they'll arrange a replacement.

First time?

You'll be added to Planning Center once you've completed this onboarding course. Make sure your contact details are up to date so you receive scheduling notifications.

Your Sunday timeline

Every Sunday follows a similar rhythm. Here's what to expect from arrival to wrap-up:

8:40
Arrive at church
8:40am is your arrival time. This gives you time to set up, collect gear, and be briefed before the doors open to the congregation.
Gear
Collect equipment
Head to the Production booth to collect the church camera (if needed) and pick up your Photo Team lanyard. Check that RAW mode is set before you go anywhere.
Brief
Shot list briefing
The Sunday Lead will brief you on the shot list for the day — any specific moments, guests, or content needs to prioritise. Listen carefully and ask questions here.
9:00
Service begins
Doors open and the service starts. You're active from this point — moving through the room, capturing moments across the full service arc.
Post
After the service
Hand your memory card or camera to the Sunday Lead for file export. They handle the transfer, cull, and edit. Your job ends here — unless you want to learn post-production!
Add a Sunday morning atmosphere photo e.g. the room filling up before service, or team setting up

The shot list

Each Sunday has a shot list — a brief set of priorities for that specific week. It might include a guest speaker to prioritise, a baptism, a special event, or a content series the communications team needs assets for.

The shot list is your guide, not a rigid script. You still have creative freedom — but make sure the priority shots are covered before you explore.

Good practice
Confirm you've got the priority shots early in the service so you're not scrambling at the end.
Good practice
If something unexpected and powerful happens — a spontaneous moment, a great reaction — capture it. Some of the best photos aren't on any shot list.
Avoid
Spending the whole service on one area and missing the full arc of the service. Move around — worship, message, connect time all need coverage.

Staying connected

The Capture Team communicates through a dedicated group chat. This is where the Lead shares reminders, schedule changes, and any special Sunday needs. Make sure you're in the group — if you're not, let the Lead know after completing this course.

If you're going to be late or need to cancel, always message the Lead directly — and do it as early as possible. It makes a big difference to coverage planning.
