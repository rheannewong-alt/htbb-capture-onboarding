Selecting your best shots

After a Sunday service you might come home with 300–600 photos. Not all of them are keepers — and that's completely normal. The key is knowing what to look for when you're reviewing your shots.

The Sunday Lead will handle the final cull and export — but as you grow in experience, it's helpful to understand the standard we're aiming for.

1
Aim for 20–30 selects
From a full Sunday, 20–30 strong photos is the target. Quality over quantity — ten excellent photos are worth more than fifty average ones.
2
Look for sharp eyes and natural expression
Eyes in focus and genuine expression are the two most important elements. A slightly soft background is fine — soft eyes are not.
3
Balance the content mix
Your selects should include wide shots, mid-range community moments, and at least a few close-up portraits. Variety makes the gallery usable for the communications team.
4
Cut duplicates ruthlessly
If you shot the same moment five times, pick the best one. The team doesn't need five versions of the same shot — they need five different moments.

The editing process

Editing and export is handled by the Sunday Lead. Once you hand over your memory card or camera, they take it from there.

The editing workflow uses Adobe Lightroom with VSCO Film presets (Film 01 and Film 05) applied to create a consistent, warm look across all photos. Finished photos are uploaded to the church's shared library and distributed to the communications team for social media, the website, and weekly updates.

Want to learn editing?

If you're interested in learning Lightroom and getting involved in the post-production side of things, let the Lead know. We'd love to develop more editors on the team — it's a valuable skill and a great way to deepen your contribution.

Copyright & photo use

All photos taken during HTBB services belong to HTBB. By volunteering on the Capture Team, you agree that photos you take on duty may be used by HTBB across its communication channels — including social media, the website, print materials, and internal communications.

You may
Share your own photos on your personal social media, with appropriate credit to HTBB where relevant. We love when the team is proud of the work they create.
You may not
Sell, license, or distribute HTBB photos to third parties — including stock photo sites — without explicit written permission from church leadership.
Never share
Unedited RAW files externally. RAW files are internal working files only. Only edited, exported JPEGs should be shared outside the team.
