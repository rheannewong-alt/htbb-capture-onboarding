Always shoot RAW

Every photo must be shot in RAW format — not JPEG. RAW files capture significantly more data than JPEGs, which gives the editing process far more flexibility to recover shadows, correct exposure, and apply presets cleanly.

Think of RAW as the negative in a film camera — JPEG is like printing from a photocopy. The original is always better.

Do this
Set your camera to RAW before every Sunday. Double-check it in the menu under Image Quality or File Format settings.
Not this
Shooting in JPEG only. JPEG files are smaller but lose critical image data — they can't be recovered in editing the same way RAW files can.

Camera settings on Sundays

Sunday services are a mixed-light environment — stage lighting can be bright and colourful while the congregation is often much darker. Here's how to approach it:

M
Manual mode (experienced photographers)
Full creative control. Start with: ISO 1600–3200, Shutter 1/100–1/200s, Aperture f/2.8 or wider. Adjust as the lighting changes through the service.
A
Auto mode (new or less experienced photographers)
Totally fine. The Sony A7 II handles auto exposure well. Use Auto ISO and let the camera do the heavy lifting while you focus on composition and timing.
★
Low-light tip for everyone
Avoid flash — always. Instead, open your aperture as wide as it goes and raise your ISO. Grain is more acceptable than blinding the congregation mid-worship.
For newer photographers

Don't stress about manual settings on your first few Sundays. Auto mode with good composition beats manual mode with bad framing every time. As you get more comfortable, you can start experimenting with manual controls.

Lightroom presets

HTBB uses two VSCO Film Lightroom presets to keep the look of our photos consistent across all Sundays and all photographers:

Primary preset
VSCO Film 01
Warm, slightly desaturated — our default look
Secondary preset
VSCO Film 05
Used for cooler or higher-contrast scenes

Presets are applied by the Sunday Lead during the editing pass — you don't need to apply them yourself. Your job is to deliver clean, well-exposed RAW files. The lead takes it from there.

Lightroom is provided. If you'd like to learn the editing process over time, speak to the lead — we'd love to have more hands in post-production.
